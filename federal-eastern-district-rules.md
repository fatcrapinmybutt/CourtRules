# U.S. District Court — Eastern District of Michigan Local Rules

> **Official Source:** [E.D. Mich. Local Rules](https://www.mied.uscourts.gov/rules/index.cfm)
> **Governing Law:** FRCP, FRCrP, FRAP, Local Rules of the Eastern District of Michigan

---

## Overview

The U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan covers:

**Northern Division:** Alcona, Alpena, Arenac, Bay, Cheboygan, Clare, Crawford, Gladwin, Huron, Iosco, Montmorency, Ogemaw, Oscoda, Otsego, Presque Isle, Roscommon, Saginaw, Tuscola

**Southern Division:** Genesee, Jackson, Lapeer, Lenawee, Livingston, Macomb, Monroe, Oakland, St. Clair, Sanilac, Shiawassee, Washtenaw, Wayne

**Court Locations:** Detroit (main), Ann Arbor, Bay City, Flint

## Local Civil Rules (LR)

### LR 1.1 — Scope; Applicability

- All civil actions filed in E.D. Mich.
- Supplement, not replace, FRCP
- Where conflict, Local Rules control

### LR 3.1 — Commencement of Action

- Civil Cover Sheet required (JS-44)
- Category designation required (e.g., automobile negligence, civil rights, contract, ERISA)
- Related case notification
- Assignment to judge by random draw

### LR 5.1 — Form of Papers; Electronic Filing

- **Format requirements:**
  - 8½ × 11 paper
  - Double-spaced body text
  - 12-point or larger font
  - 1-inch margins
  - Page numbers
- **CM/ECF mandatory** for all attorneys
- Electronic service via NEF upon filing
- Pro se filers use paper unless court orders otherwise

### LR 5.2 — Privacy Protection

- Social Security numbers: last 4 digits only
- Financial account numbers: last 4 digits only
- Dates of birth: year only
- Names of minors: initials only (E.D. Mich. uses full initials, not just first initial)
- Home addresses: city and state only
- Attorneys responsible for redaction before filing

### LR 7.1 — Motion Practice

- **Subrule (a):** Movant must seek concurrence
  - If opponent concurs → stipulated order
  - If opponent does not concur → state in motion
- **Subrule (b):** Briefs
  - Brief in support must accompany all dispositive motions
  - Optional for non-dispositive motions (but recommended)
- **Subrule (c):** Page limits
  - Brief supporting motion: **25 pages** maximum
  - Response brief: **20 pages** maximum
  - Reply brief: **7 pages** maximum
  - Exceeding page limits requires leave of court
- **Subrule (d):** Timing
  - Response due **21 days** after service of motion (civil)
  - Reply due **14 days** after service of response
- **Subrule (e):** Hearing
  - Court may decide on briefs alone
  - Oral argument available upon request; court has discretion
- **Subrule (f):** Motion for Reconsideration
  - Must be filed within **14 days** of order or judgment
  - Must demonstrate "palpable defect" in prior decision
  - Correcting that defect must result in different disposition
  - **3-page limit**
  - No new arguments

### LR 7.2 — Summary Judgment

- Non-movant must file
  - Response brief
  - Statement of Genuine Issues identifying which facts are disputed
- Each statement of material fact requires record citation
- Failure to respond may result in grant of motion

### LR 11.1 — Signing Documents

- Applies to all papers filed
- Attorney of record certifies accuracy
- Electronic signature accepted

### LR 15.1 — Amended Pleadings

- Motion to amend must attach proposed amended pleading
- Redlined version showing changes must also be attached

### LR 16.1 — Scheduling

- Scheduling order issued after FRCP 26(f) conference
- Scheduling order sets all case deadlines
- Modification requires showing good cause (FRCP 16(b)(4))

### LR 16.2 — Alternative Dispute Resolution

- E.D. Mich. has robust ADR program
  - Case Evaluation (binding arbitration-type process)
  - Mediation
  - Early Neutral Evaluation
  - Settlement conferences with magistrate judge
- Court may order ADR participation

### LR 26.1 — Mandatory Disclosures

- FRCP 26(a)(1) initial disclosures required within 14 days of 26(f) conference
- Expert witness disclosures per scheduling order
- Rebuttal experts: 30 days after initial expert disclosure

### LR 26.2 — Discovery Disputes

- Mandatory meet and confer before filing motion
- Certificate of compliance with conferral required
- Magistrate judges typically resolve discovery disputes in E.D. Mich.

### LR 26.3 — ESI (Electronically Stored Information)

- Parties should address ESI in Rule 26(f) conference
- Disputes about ESI preservation, collection, format resolved by court
- Cooperation expected; courts may sanction intentional spoliation

### LR 37.1 — Motion to Compel

- Must certify meet and confer efforts
- Describe each dispute separately
- Court may award fees under FRCP 37

### LR 40.1 — Trial Scheduling

- Trial date set at scheduling conference
- Continuances granted only for good cause

### LR 41.1 — Dismissal for Want of Prosecution

- Cases inactive for **1 year** may be dismissed
- Order to show cause issued first with 28-day response period

### LR 47.1 — Jury Selection

- Voir dire — judge conducts with attorney participation
- Peremptory challenges per FRCP 47
- Batson challenges to discriminatory peremptories available

### LR 54.1 — Bill of Costs

- Filed within **28 days** of judgment
- Clerk of Court taxes costs
- Objections to bill of costs filed within 14 days

### LR 54.1.1 — Attorneys' Fees

- Motion filed within **14 days** of judgment
- Must include itemized time records
- Billing rate must be supported by affidavit or evidence

### LR 56.1 — Summary Judgment Procedure

- Moving party files statement of material facts
- Numbered paragraphs with citations to record
- Non-moving party must respond paragraph by paragraph
- "Material facts" not genuinely disputed deemed admitted

### LR 65.1 — Injunctions and Temporary Restraining Orders

- TRO ex parte — stringent showing required
- Must comply with FRCP 65(b)(1)
- Notice required unless irreparable harm shown
- Bond typically required

### LR 72.1 — Magistrate Judges — Non-Dispositive Matters

- Referred by district judge
- Objections filed within **14 days** of order
- Standard: clearly erroneous or contrary to law

### LR 72.2 — Magistrate Judges — Dispositive Matters

- Report and Recommendation (R&R) issued
- Objections filed within **14 days**
- District judge reviews de novo

### LR 83.20 — Attorneys — Admission to Bar

- Must be member of State Bar of Michigan or admitted pro hac vice
- Annual dues
- Disciplinary authority of court

### LR 83.21 — Pro Hac Vice Admission

- Motion by local counsel
- Must accompany all court appearances
- Fee required

### LR 83.22 — Pro Se Parties

- Bound by same rules as attorneys
- Pleadings liberally construed
- Court may appoint counsel in forma pauperis cases under 28 U.S.C. § 1915

### LR 83.25 — Attorney Withdrawal

- Motion required; must show client notified
- Court reluctant to grant withdrawal close to trial

## Local Criminal Rules (LCrR)

### LCrR 1.1 — Applicability

- All criminal proceedings in E.D. Mich.

### LCrR 5.1 — Initial Appearance

- Without unnecessary delay after arrest (FRCrP 5)
- Magistrate judge presides
- Defendant advised of rights, charges, counsel options

### LCrR 6.1 — Grand Jury Secrecy

- Grand jury materials sealed
- Disclosure only by court order

### LCrR 10.1 — Arraignment Procedures

- Scheduling order entered
- Plea entered
- Discovery ordered

### LCrR 12.1 — Pretrial Motions

- Filed by deadline in scheduling order
- Briefing schedule set by court
- Evidentiary hearings if required

### LCrR 16.1 — Discovery

- Government's Brady/Giglio obligations addressed at arraignment
- Defendant's Rule 16 obligations reciprocal
- ESI discovery increasingly common in white-collar cases

### LCrR 32.1 — Sentencing

- PSR provided to parties at least **35 days** before sentencing
- Objections to PSR due at least **14 days** before sentencing
- Sentencing memoranda filed at least **7 days** before sentencing
- Court applies U.S. Sentencing Guidelines (18 U.S.C. § 3553(a) factors)

### LCrR 45.1 — Time Computation

- Consistent with FRCrP 45 and FRCP 6

## Special Programs in E.D. Mich.

- **Detroit Federal Courthouse (Theodore Levin U.S. Courthouse)**
  - Largest docket — Wayne County cases
- **Flint Federal Court**
  - Genesee County cases
- **Ann Arbor Federal Court**
  - Washtenaw County cases
- **Business Court Program**
  - Complex commercial litigation — specialized judges
- **Drug Treatment Court**
  - Diversion program for eligible defendants
- **Prisoner Civil Rights Cases (§ 1983)**
  - Screened under 28 U.S.C. § 1915A before service
  - Three-strikes rule: 28 U.S.C. § 1915(g)
