## Part II — Court of Appeals (MCR 7.200–7.226)

### MCR 7.201 — Organization and Operation of Court
- Subrule (A): Sessions and locations
  - Detroit session (Wayne County)
  - Lansing session (Ingham, Eaton, Clinton, etc.)
  - Grand Rapids session (western Michigan)
- Subrule (B): Composition of panels — 3 judges
- Subrule (C): Presiding judge
- Subrule (D): En banc proceedings
  - When en banc review may be sought
  - Procedures for en banc consideration
  - Effect of en banc decision
- Subrule (E): Clerk's office
- Subrule (F): Sessions of court

### MCR 7.202 — Definitions
- "Claim of appeal"
- "Delayed appeal"
- "Final judgment or order"
  - Civil cases: judgment resolving all claims
  - Criminal cases: judgment of conviction and sentence
- "Interlocutory order"
- "Leave to appeal"
- "Motion for reconsideration"

### MCR 7.203 — Jurisdiction
- Subrule (A): Appeals of right
  - Subrule (A)(1): Final judgments or orders from circuit courts (except as exempted)
  - Subrule (A)(2): Final orders of probate courts
  - Subrule (A)(3): Orders granting or refusing new trial, judgment notwithstanding verdict (in criminal cases, prosecution has limited appeal rights)
  - Subrule (A)(4): Orders granting/denying motions affecting substantial rights in criminal proceedings
  - Subrule (A)(5): Appeals from administrative agencies as provided by statute
  - Subrule (B)(1): Leave to appeal — interlocutory orders
  - Subrule (B)(2): Leave to appeal — orders not otherwise appealable
  - Subrule (B)(3): Leave to appeal — discretionary in all other cases
  - Subrule (C): Superintending control — original actions over lower courts/tribunals
  - Subrule (D): Cross-appeals

### MCR 7.204 — Filing Appeal of Right; Appearance
- Subrule (A): Time for filing
  - **21 days** from entry of final order in civil cases **(amended — now 21 days)**
  - **21 days** from entry of judgment of conviction and sentencing in criminal cases
  - Exception: where statute provides different period
- Subrule (B): Form of claim of appeal
  - Appellant's name and address
  - Court appealed from; case number
  - Judgment or order appealed
  - Statement of jurisdiction
  - Signature of appellant or counsel
- Subrule (C): Filing with trial court
- Subrule (D): Effect of filing — stays enforcement unless court orders otherwise
- Subrule (E): Payment of fees
- Subrule (F): Transcript orders — must order within 7 days of filing claim

### MCR 7.205 — Application for Leave to Appeal
- Subrule (A): When filed — applications for leave
  - Within **21 days** of entry of order
  - Delayed applications — within **12 months** with showing of good cause
- Subrule (B): Content of application
  - Table of contents
  - Statement of questions presented
  - Statement of facts
  - Argument
  - Appendix with copies of pertinent orders
- Subrule (C): Response to application
  - Appellee has **21 days** to respond
- Subrule (D): Ruling on application
  - Grant, deny, or peremptory disposition
- Subrule (E): Delayed application
  - Must show (1) good cause for delay and (2) merits of appeal
- Subrule (F): Stay pending appeal
- Subrule (G): Cross-applications

### MCR 7.206 — Interlocutory Appeals; Original Actions; Appointment of Counsel
- Subrule (A): Interlocutory leave to appeal
  - Standards: interlocutory appeal warranted only if issue is of significant public interest, or party would suffer substantial harm if forced to await final judgment
- Subrule (B): Original actions (mandamus, prohibition, habeas corpus, quo warranto)
  - Complaint filed with COA
  - Response required within 21 days
  - Oral argument may be held
- Subrule (C): Emergency proceedings
- Subrule (D): Appointment of counsel
  - Court may appoint counsel in civil cases under appropriate circumstances
  - In criminal cases per MCR 6.425(F)

### MCR 7.207 — Cross-Appeal
- Subrule (A): Time for cross-appeal — within **21 days** of appellant's claim of appeal
- Subrule (B): Form
- Subrule (C): Briefing schedule affected by cross-appeal

### MCR 7.208 — Stay of Proceedings; Bond
- Subrule (A): Automatic stay in civil cases — not automatic
- Subrule (B): Stay by trial court
- Subrule (C): Stay by Court of Appeals
- Subrule (D): Bond or surety requirements
- Subrule (E): Criminal cases — bail pending appeal

### MCR 7.209 — Record on Appeal
- Subrule (A): Composition of record
  - All pleadings, motions, orders, judgments filed below
  - All exhibits submitted at trial or hearing
  - Transcript of proceedings
- Subrule (B): Duty to order transcript
  - Appellant must order transcript promptly
- Subrule (C): Statement of facts in lieu of transcript
  - Agreed statement of facts when transcript unavailable
- Subrule (D): Correction of record
- Subrule (E): Filing with Court of Appeals
- Subrule (F): Record on incomplete or lost transcript

### MCR 7.210 — Briefs
- Subrule (A): Required briefs
  - Appellant's brief
  - Appellee's brief
  - Appellant's reply brief (optional)
- Subrule (B): Time for filing
  - Appellant's brief: **56 days** after entry of claim of appeal (or after transcript is filed, if later)
  - Appellee's brief: **35 days** after appellant's brief is served
  - Reply brief: **21 days** after appellee's brief is served
- Subrule (C): Content requirements
  - Table of contents with page references
  - Table of authorities
  - Statement of questions presented (issues on appeal)
  - Statement of facts
  - Argument — organized by issue, with citations
  - Relief requested
- Subrule (D): Length limits
  - Principal brief: **50 pages** (or 16,000 words)
  - Reply brief: **10 pages** (or 3,200 words)
- Subrule (E): Format
  - 12-point or larger font
  - 1-inch margins
  - Double-spaced text
  - Page numbers
- Subrule (F): Signature and certification
- Subrule (G): Appendix

### MCR 7.211 — Motions in Court of Appeals
- Subrule (A): Form and filing
- Subrule (B): Response
  - **7 days** to respond unless court orders otherwise
- Subrule (C): Types of motions
  - Motion to dismiss appeal
  - Motion to extend time
  - Motion for peremptory disposition
  - Motion for oral argument
  - Motion to consolidate
  - Motion to seal record
  - Motion for immediate consideration
- Subrule (D): Emergency motions
- Subrule (E): Reconsideration motions
  - Must be filed within **21 days** of decision

### MCR 7.212 — Briefs in Calendar Cases
- Subrule (A): Calendar cases — cases set for submission or argument
- Subrule (B): Briefing schedule
- Subrule (C): Content requirements (same as MCR 7.210(C))
- Subrule (D): Amicus curiae briefs
  - Permission required
  - Must not repeat arguments in principal brief

### MCR 7.213 — Calendar Cases
- Subrule (A): Assignment to calendar
- Subrule (B): Session assignments
- Subrule (C): Submission without oral argument
- Subrule (D): Oral argument
  - 15 minutes per side (default)
  - Additional time by motion
- Subrule (E): Supplemental briefs
  - Leave required
  - Must identify new authority or changed circumstances

### MCR 7.214 — Dismissal; Default
- Subrule (A): Voluntary dismissal — any time before submission
- Subrule (B): Involuntary dismissal
  - Failure to file brief timely
  - Failure to pay fees
  - Failure to prosecute
- Subrule (C): Default — appellee's default
- Subrule (D): Reinstatement after dismissal/default

### MCR 7.215 — Opinions, Orders, and Judgments
- Subrule (A): Decisions by opinion or order
- Subrule (B): Publication of opinions
  - Published opinions are binding precedent
  - Unpublished opinions are not binding but may be cited for persuasive value
- Subrule (C): Entry of judgment
- Subrule (D): Mandate — **21 days** after entry of opinion, unless stayed
- Subrule (E): Costs

### MCR 7.216 — Miscellaneous Relief
- Subrule (A): Authority of Court of Appeals to
  - Exercise jurisdiction and powers
  - Grant any relief the case warrants
  - Remand for additional proceedings
  - Make factual determinations
- Subrule (B): Summary disposition of appeals

### MCR 7.217 — Court of Appeals — Reconsideration
- Must be filed within **21 days** of opinion/order
- Must identify point of law or fact overlooked or misapprehended
- No new arguments unless extraordinary circumstances
- Response not required unless ordered
- Decision typically without oral argument

### MCR 7.218 — Court of Appeals — Special Panel
- When three-judge panel disagrees with prior published opinion
- Conflict must be identified
- Special seven-judge conflict resolution panel

### MCR 7.219 — Taxation of Costs
- Prevailing party may tax costs
- Taxable costs include filing fees, transcript costs, printing/copying
- Motion for costs filed with clerk

### MCR 7.220 — Bonds
- Supersedeas bond
- Security bond for costs

### MCR 7.221 — Workers' Compensation Appeals
- Jurisdiction: orders of Workers' Compensation Appellate Commission
- Time: **20 days** from order

### MCR 7.222 — Criminal Appeals — Indigent Defendants
- Appointment of appellate counsel
- Counsel must perfect appeal and file required briefs
- Anders brief procedures (when counsel finds no meritorious issues)

### MCR 7.223 — Appeals from Administrative Agencies
- Subrule (A): Jurisdiction conferred by statute
- Subrule (B): Filing requirements
- Subrule (C): Record from agency
- Subrule (D): Briefing
- Subrule (E): Standard of review

### MCR 7.224 — DNA Appeals
- Post-conviction DNA testing appeals
- Expedited schedule

### MCR 7.225 — Civil Appeals — Expedited Proceedings
- Specified categories of cases may be expedited
- Shorter briefing deadlines

### MCR 7.226 — Pro Per Criminal Appeals
- Procedures for defendants appearing without counsel on appeal
- Court may appoint counsel if substantial issues exist

## Part III — Supreme Court Appellate Rules (MCR 7.300–7.326)

### MCR 7.301 — Jurisdiction of Supreme Court
- Subrule (A)(1): Leave to appeal from COA decisions
- Subrule (A)(2): Superintending control over all courts
- Subrule (A)(3): Original jurisdiction — writs of mandamus, prohibition, habeas corpus, quo warranto, certiorari
- Subrule (A)(4): Advisory opinions to Governor (Const 1963, Art 6, § 8)
- Subrule (B): Bypass of COA — direct appeal from circuit court
- Subrule (C): Certification from federal courts

### MCR 7.302 — Applications for Leave to Appeal to Supreme Court
- Subrule (A): Who may apply — any party aggrieved by COA decision
- Subrule (B): Time limits
  - Civil: **56 days** from COA opinion/order
  - Criminal: **56 days** from COA opinion/order
  - Delayed application: within **6 months**, with good cause
- Subrule (C): Content of application
  - Concise statement of facts
  - Argument why leave should be granted
  - Copy of COA opinion
  - Copy of trial court judgment
  - Tables of contents and authorities
- Subrule (D): Grounds for granting leave
  - Conflict with Supreme Court decisions
  - Conflict between COA opinions
  - Question of significant public interest
  - Substantial departures from controlling law
  - Significant constitutional question
- Subrule (E): Response to application
  - Appellee has **28 days**
- Subrule (F): Cross-application
  - Must be filed within **21 days** of original application
- Subrule (G): Oral argument on application — rarely granted

### MCR 7.303 — Actions Originating in Supreme Court
- Original writs
- Complaint filed with Clerk of Supreme Court
- Respondent has 28 days to file answer

### MCR 7.304 — Bypass of Court of Appeals
- Application filed directly with Supreme Court
- Within **42 days** of final judgment in circuit court
- Court considers whether case involves issue of significant public interest or broad legal significance

### MCR 7.305 — Supreme Court Applications — Leave to Appeal
- Subrule (C)(2)(a): Civil — 56 days from COA decision
- Subrule (C)(2)(b): Criminal — 56 days
- Subrule (C)(4): Delayed application — 6-month window with good cause

### MCR 7.306 — Supreme Court — Brief Requirements
- Identical format requirements to COA (MCR 7.210(C))
- Page limits: **50 pages** for principal briefs; **25 pages** for reply
- Amicus curiae briefs require permission of Court

### MCR 7.307 — Supreme Court — Oral Argument
- 30 minutes per side (default for leave granted cases)
- Additional time by motion
- Calendar cases set by Clerk

### MCR 7.308 — Supreme Court Opinions and Orders
- Opinions are binding on all Michigan courts
- Concurrences, dissents
- Remand orders

### MCR 7.309 — Supreme Court — Reconsideration
- Motion for reconsideration within **21 days** of opinion
- Must show substantial grounds — rare grant

### MCR 7.310 — Supreme Court — Mandate
- Issues after reconsideration period expires (21 days) unless stayed

### MCR 7.311 — Certification from Federal Courts
- Supreme Court may accept certified questions from:
  - U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit
  - U.S. District Courts (Eastern and Western Districts)
- Procedure: federal court certifies question of Michigan law
- Supreme Court may accept or decline
- Briefing and argument follow standard rules

### MCR 7.312 — Superintending Control
- Used to compel lower courts to perform legal duty
- Available when no adequate remedy by appeal
- Filed as complaint in Supreme Court

### MCR 7.313 — Advisory Opinions (Michigan Const 1963, Art 6, § 8)
- Governor may request advisory opinion on constitutionality of legislation
- Not binding law, but highly persuasive

### MCR 7.315 — Cases Decided by Stipulation
- Parties may agree to disposition

### MCR 7.316 — Dismissal in Supreme Court
- Voluntary — before submission
- Involuntary — failure to prosecute

### MCR 7.317 — Taxation of Costs in Supreme Court
- Prevailing party may tax costs

### MCR 7.318 — Record on Appeal in Supreme Court
- Same record transmitted from COA
- Parties may request supplemental record

### MCR 7.319 — Stay of Proceedings Pending Supreme Court Appeal
- Application to COA or Supreme Court

### MCR 7.320 — Amicus Curiae in Supreme Court
- Motion for permission to file
- Must state interest of amicus
- May not repeat arguments of parties

### MCR 7.321 — Expedited Appeals in Supreme Court
- Available in cases of great public urgency

### MCR 7.323 — Opinions and Orders — Publication Policy
- All Supreme Court opinions published
- Unpublished orders not binding but may be cited

### MCR 7.325 — Mandate on Reversal or Remand
- Directions to lower court on remand
- Scope of proceedings on remand

### MCR 7.326 — Cross-Appeal to Supreme Court
- Filed within 21 days of original application

## Summary: Key Deadlines at a Glance

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Claim of appeal (civil) from circuit court to COA | 21 days from entry of order |
| Application for leave to appeal to COA | 21 days (delayed: 12 months) |
| Appellant's brief (COA) | 56 days after claim of appeal filed |
| Appellee's brief (COA) | 35 days after appellant's brief served |
| Reply brief (COA) | 21 days after appellee's brief served |
| Motion for reconsideration (COA) | 21 days from opinion |
| Application for leave to appeal to Supreme Court | 56 days from COA decision |
| Delayed application to Supreme Court | Within 6 months, with good cause |
| Bypass application to Supreme Court | 42 days from circuit court judgment |
| Cross-application to Supreme Court | 21 days from original application |
