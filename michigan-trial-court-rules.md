# Michigan Trial Court Rules Overview (MCR 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 — Key Provisions)

This document provides a quick-reference overview of Michigan trial court procedural rules most relevant to civil, domestic relations, and criminal litigation in circuit, probate, and district courts.

> For the full cascading list of all MCR rules see: [Michigan Supreme Court Rules — Full List](michigan-supreme-court-rules.md)

---

## Circuit Court Civil Procedure Highlights

### Commencing a Civil Action

- **Complaint** — MCR 2.110, 2.111
  - Short and plain statement of facts and each count
  - Relief demanded
  - Signed by attorney or pro se party

- **Summons** — MCR 2.102
  - Issued by court clerk upon filing
  - Valid for **91 days** from issuance
  - Reissuable; case dismissed if not served within time

- **Service of Process** — MCR 2.105
  - Personal service (preferred)
  - Residence service (leaving copy with adult at dwelling)
  - Registered agent service (corporations)
  - Publication/posting (last resort, requires court order — MCR 2.106)

- **Answer** — MCR 2.108
  - Due **21 days** after service of complaint
  - Admit or deny each allegation
  - Affirmative defenses must be raised in answer

### Discovery Highlights

| Tool | Rule | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interrogatories | MCR 2.309 | Max 20 unless court allows more |
| Depositions | MCR 2.306 | Notice required; officer must administer oath |
| Document Requests | MCR 2.310 | 28 days to respond |
| Admissions | MCR 2.312 | 28 days to respond; deemed admitted if no response |
| Physical/Mental Exam | MCR 2.311 | Order required; good cause shown |
| Subpoena | MCR 2.506 | Can compel non-party attendance and production |

### Motion to Dismiss / Summary Disposition — MCR 2.116

| Subrule | Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| C(1) | No personal jurisdiction | Pre-answer motion |
| C(2) | No subject matter jurisdiction | Raised at any time |
| C(7) | Statute of limitations, immunity, release | Can be raised by motion |
| C(8) | Failure to state claim (pleadings) | No evidence considered |
| C(10) | No genuine issue of material fact | Evidence considered; burden shifts |

### Trial Procedure

- **Jury demand** — MCR 2.508 — must be made within 28 days of answer
- **Jury size** — 6 jurors in circuit court civil cases (MCR 2.510)
- **Peremptory challenges** — 3 per side in civil cases
- **Instructions** — submitted before final argument (MCR 2.512)
- **Verdict** — general, special, or general with interrogatories (MCR 2.514)
- **JNOV motion** — within **21 days** of verdict (MCR 2.610)
- **New trial motion** — within **21 days** of verdict (MCR 2.611)

## Domestic Relations Procedure Highlights (MCR 3.200 Series)

### Divorce / Custody Case Procedure

1. **Complaint** filed — MCR 3.206
   - Must include jurisdictional allegations
   - Must state date of marriage, names of minor children, property summary
2. **Summons** issued — MCR 3.204
3. **Answer** due — 21 days from service
4. **Automatic temporary restraining order (ATRO)** — in many counties upon filing
5. **FOC investigation** — Friend of the Court evaluates custody/parenting time (MCR 3.208)
6. **Referee hearing** — MCR 3.209
   - Referee hears contested issues, issues recommendation
   - Parties may object within **21 days** of referee's recommendation
7. **Trial** — before circuit judge on contested issues
8. **Judgment of Divorce** — MCR 3.211

### Custody Modification — MCR 3.212

- Must establish **proper cause or change of circumstances** (*Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003))
- Then determine whether modification is in **best interests of child** (MCL 722.23)
- **Established custodial environment** — changes to ECE require **clear and convincing evidence**
- Changes to parenting time (without ECE change) require **preponderance of evidence**

### Best Interests Factors (MCL 722.23)

- (a) Love, affection, and other emotional ties between parties and child
- (b) Capacity to give child love, affection, guidance; continuation of education and religion
- (c) Capacity to provide material needs
- (d) Length of time child has lived in stable home environment
- (e) Permanence of family unit
- (f) Moral fitness of parties
- (g) Mental and physical health of parties
- (h) Home, school, community record of child
- (i) Child's reasonable preference (depending on age and maturity)
- (j) Willingness to facilitate relationship with other parent *(parental alienation factor)*
- (k) Domestic violence
- (l) Any other relevant factor

### Friend of the Court (FOC) — MCR 3.208

- Investigates custody/parenting time disputes
- Issues report and recommendation
- Parties may object within **21 days**
- Objections trigger de novo hearing before circuit judge or referee

### Personal Protection Orders (PPO) — MCR 3.310

- **Ex parte PPO** — issued without notice if immediate harm shown
- Effective immediately upon issuance
- Respondent may file motion to modify or terminate within **14 days** of service
- **Contempt** for violation: criminal contempt charges under MCL 764.15b

## Criminal Procedure Highlights (MCR 6)

### Pre-Trial Timeline (Felony)

| Stage | Rule | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Arrest | MCR 6.103 | Must arraign promptly (MCR 6.104) |
| Arraignment on complaint | MCR 6.104 | Bail set; counsel appointed |
| Preliminary examination | MCR 6.110 | Within 14 days of arraignment (if not waived) |
| Arraignment on information | MCR 6.113 | After bindover |
| Pretrial motions deadline | MCR 6.110 | Set by court order |
| Trial | MCR 6.401 | Jury trial right available |
| Sentencing | MCR 6.425 | After conviction |

### Speedy Trial — MCR 6.004

- Michigan: 180-day rule (MCL 780.131) for prisoners
- Constitutional speedy trial rights (Barker v Wingo factors)
  - Length of delay
  - Reason for delay
  - Defendant's assertion of right
  - Prejudice to defendant

### Discovery in Criminal Cases — MCR 6.201

**Prosecutor must provide:**
- Police reports, witness statements
- Defendant's statements
- Scientific evidence; expert reports
- Exculpatory evidence (Brady/Giglio material)
- Criminal history of prosecution witnesses (Giglio)
- List of prosecution witnesses

**Defendant must provide (after prosecutor's disclosure):**
- Defense witnesses list
- Expert witness information
- Alibi witnesses

### Sentencing — MCR 6.425

- **Presentence Investigation Report (PSIR)** prepared by probation
- Minimum sentencing guidelines: MCL 769.34
- Michigan Sentencing Guidelines grid — determines minimum sentence range
- Court must score guidelines; departure requires substantial and compelling reason
- Victim impact statements at sentencing
- Defendant has right to allocution (speak before sentencing)

### Post-Conviction Relief — MCR 6.501

**Motion for Relief from Judgment (MRJ):**
- Available for claims not raised on direct appeal with good cause
- Grounds: newly discovered evidence, ineffective assistance of counsel, constitutional violations
- Standard: "good cause" for failure to raise + "actual prejudice"
- Successive motions restricted

## District Court Highlights (MCR 4)

### Small Claims Court — MCR 4.101

- Jurisdiction: claims up to **$7,000** (effective 2022)
- No attorneys allowed without court permission
- Informal hearing before district judge or magistrate
- No appeals as of right (only on questions of law)

### Summary Proceedings (Landlord-Tenant) — MCR 4.201

- Used for evictions / recovery of possession
- Summary Complaint filed; summons issued
- Hearing within **10–14 days** of service
- Writ of restitution if judgment for landlord
- **Stay of writ** may be granted for payment of rent owed

## Probate Court Highlights (MCR 5)

### Estate Administration

- Informal proceedings: no notice required for many actions
- Formal proceedings: notice to interested persons
- Claims against estate — must be filed within time in notice to creditors
- Personal Representative (PR) duties governed by EPIC (MCL 700.1101 et seq.)

### Guardianship / Conservatorship — MCR 5.401

- Petition filed describing alleged incapacity
- Court-appointed visitor assesses proposed ward
- Hearing with right to counsel
- Annual reviews required
- **Least restrictive alternatives** must be considered
