# U.S. Court of Appeals — Sixth Circuit Rules

> **Official Source:** [Sixth Circuit Rules and I.O.P.s](https://www.ca6.uscourts.gov/rules-and-procedures)
> Covers: Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee

---

## Overview

The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit hears appeals from the Eastern and Western Districts of Michigan, as well as the federal district courts in Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee.

- **Headquarters:** Potter Stewart U.S. Courthouse, 100 E. Fifth Street, Cincinnati, OH 45202
- **Michigan cases** typically argued in Cincinnati or occasionally by video

---

## Sixth Circuit Local Rules (6 Cir. R.)

### 6 Cir. R. 10 — The Record on Appeal

- **Rule 10(a):** Record defined — all documents, exhibits, transcripts from district court
- **Rule 10(b):** Appellant must order transcript within **14 days** of filing notice of appeal
  - Failure to order transcript → possible dismissal
- **Rule 10(c):** Statement of the evidence if transcript unavailable
- **Rule 10(d):** Correction or modification of record
- **Rule 10(e):** Sealed materials — require special handling

### 6 Cir. R. 11 — Forwarding the Record

- District court clerk forwards record to Circuit Clerk
- Electronic record transmission standard

### 6 Cir. R. 12 — Docketing and Fees

- **Rule 12(a):** Notice of appeal filed in district court triggers docketing
- **Rule 12(b):** Filing fees — $505 (2024 rate) paid to district court at time of notice
- **Rule 12(c):** In forma pauperis — motion to proceed without prepayment of fees

### 6 Cir. R. 25 — Filing and Service

- Electronic filing (ECF) mandatory for all attorneys
- Pro se filers may use paper
- All filings must include certificate of service

### 6 Cir. R. 26 — Computing and Extending Time

- FRAP 26 governs time computation
- Clerk's office open Monday–Friday 8:30 am–5:00 pm Eastern
- Motions to extend time must show good cause

### 6 Cir. R. 26.1 — Corporate Disclosure Statement

- All parties (other than individuals and government entities) must file
- Identifies any parent corporation and any publicly held company owning 10%+ of stock
- Filed with first document in court

### 6 Cir. R. 27 — Motions

- **Rule 27(a):** Form — must comply with FRAP 27 and local rules
- **Rule 27(b):** Emergency motions
  - Must contact opposing counsel and clerk's office before filing
  - Brief explanation of emergency required
- **Rule 27(c):** Motions to dismiss
  - Within **14 days** of when appellee's brief would be due
- **Rule 27(d):** Motions for stay pending appeal
  - Four-factor standard: likelihood of success on merits, irreparable harm, harm to others, public interest

### 6 Cir. R. 28 — Briefs

- **Rule 28(a):** Content of Appellant's Brief
  - Corporate disclosure statement
  - Table of contents (with page references)
  - Table of authorities
  - Statement regarding oral argument
  - Jurisdictional statement
    - Basis for district court's jurisdiction (statute, amount in controversy)
    - Basis for circuit court's jurisdiction
    - Timeliness of appeal
    - Final or appealable order statement
  - Statement of issues presented for review
  - Statement of the case (procedural history and facts)
  - Summary of argument
  - Argument (with headings for each issue)
  - Conclusion (precise relief sought)
  - Certificate of compliance
  - Certificate of service
- **Rule 28(b):** Appellee's Brief — same format requirements
- **Rule 28(c):** Reply Brief — limited to responding to appellee's arguments
- **Rule 28(d):** Length limits
  - Principal briefs: **14,000 words** (or 30 pages if non-proportional font)
  - Reply brief: **7,000 words** (or 15 pages)
- **Rule 28(e):** Citations to record
  - All factual assertions must cite to record (transcript page, document number)
  - Cite appendix: "App. ___"
- **Rule 28(f):** Appendix requirements

### 6 Cir. R. 28.1 — Timing of Briefs

- Appellant's brief: **40 days** after record is filed (or docketing of appeal)
- Appellee's brief: **30 days** after appellant's brief served
- Reply brief: **21 days** after appellee's brief served
- Extension by motion showing good cause

### 6 Cir. R. 29 — Amicus Curiae Briefs

- Permission required unless filed by government
- Motion to file amicus must state interest and how brief differs from parties'
- Same format requirements as party briefs
- Due at same time as brief of party being supported

### 6 Cir. R. 30 — Appendix to Briefs

- Appellant must file appendix with opening brief
- **Required in appendix:**
  - District court docket entries
  - Relevant portions of pleadings, motions
  - Final judgment or order being appealed
  - All orders relevant to appeal
  - Relevant jury instructions
  - Relevant trial exhibits
- Sealed materials — filed separately as confidential appendix
- Deferred appendix procedure available

### 6 Cir. R. 31 — Filing the Briefs

- All parties must file **6 paper copies** (plus electronic) in cases set for argument
- Cases decided on briefs: **4 paper copies**
- Binding: bound on left side or saddle-stitched

### 6 Cir. R. 32 — Form of Briefs, Appendices, and Other Papers

- Font: **14-point** proportionally spaced or **12-point** monospaced
- Double-spaced body
- 1-inch margins
- Page numbers centered at bottom
- Cover page identifying:
  - Court name and case number
  - Names of parties
  - Title of brief
  - Name of attorney/party filing
- Cover colors:
  - Appellant: **blue**
  - Appellee: **red**
  - Intervenor/amicus supporting appellant: **green**
  - Intervenor/amicus supporting appellee: **yellow**
  - Appellant's reply: **gray**

### 6 Cir. R. 33 — Settlement Conferences and Mediation

- Sixth Circuit has a formal mediation program
- Most civil appeals are screened for mediation
- Mediation is confidential; mediators are neutral
- Court may order mediation

### 6 Cir. R. 34 — Oral Argument

- **Rule 34(a):** Presumption of oral argument in most cases
- **Rule 34(b):** Cases submitted on briefs (no argument):
  - Frivolous appeals
  - Purely legal questions adequately briefed
  - Cases recently authoritatively decided
- **Rule 34(c):** Time for argument
  - Standard allocation: **15 minutes** per side
  - Complex cases: up to **30 minutes** per side
  - Divided argument (multiple appellants): allocated among counsel
- **Rule 34(d):** Conduct of argument
  - Counsel should know record
  - Court may interrupt with questions
  - No reading from brief
  - Save rebuttal time (announce at outset)

### 6 Cir. R. 35 — En Banc Consideration

- **Rule 35(a):** When en banc review warranted
  - Overruling circuit precedent
  - Securing uniformity of circuit decisions
  - Resolving question of exceptional importance
- **Rule 35(b):** Petition for en banc rehearing
  - Filed within **45 days** of judgment in civil cases
  - Filed within **14 days** in criminal cases
  - Must specifically identify which panel opinion conflicts with prior decision or raises exceptional importance
  - Maximum **15 pages**
- **Rule 35(c):** Response
  - No response unless ordered by court
- **Rule 35(d):** Vote on en banc rehearing
  - Majority of active judges in regular active service

### 6 Cir. R. 36 — Opinions and Judgments

- **Rule 36(a):** Precedential opinions — published opinions bind later panels
- **Rule 36(b):** Unpublished opinions — may be cited for persuasive value only (since 2007, per FRAP 32.1)
- **Rule 36(c):** Judgment entry — effective when entered on docket

### 6 Cir. R. 40 — Petition for Panel Rehearing

- Filed within **45 days** of judgment (civil) or **14 days** (criminal)
- Must identify points of law or fact overlooked or misapprehended
- Maximum **15 pages**
- No response unless ordered
- Grant of rehearing — new briefing may be ordered

### 6 Cir. R. 41 — Mandate

- Issues **7 days** after expiration of time to petition for certiorari (90 days)
- Or after denial of certiorari
- Recall of mandate available in extraordinary circumstances

### 6 Cir. R. 45 — Clerks

- Clerk's office open Monday–Friday
- All requests for extensions must be in writing

### 6 Cir. R. 47 — Attorney Admission

- Must be member of Supreme Court bar or admitted on motion
- Application, fee, sponsor required
- Reciprocal admission available for practitioners from other circuits

## Criminal Appeals — Specific Rules

- Notice of appeal filed within **14 days** of judgment (FRAP 4(b))
- Government's cross-appeal within **30 days**
- Bail pending appeal governed by 18 U.S.C. § 3143 and FRAP 9
- Anders briefing procedure for appointed counsel finding no non-frivolous issues

---

## Habeas Corpus Appeals (28 U.S.C. § 2254 / § 2255)

- Certificate of Appealability (COA) required
  - Issued by district court or circuit court
  - Petitioner must show "substantial showing of the denial of a constitutional right" (28 U.S.C. § 2253(c))
- Briefing schedule same as civil cases
- Petition for certiorari to U.S. Supreme Court within **90 days** of Sixth Circuit judgment

---

## Appeals to U.S. Supreme Court from Sixth Circuit

- Petition for Writ of Certiorari filed within **90 days** of judgment
- Filed with U.S. Supreme Court Clerk
- Supreme Court Rules govern (not Sixth Circuit rules)
- See: [Litigating Higher Courts Guide](litigating-higher-courts.md)
