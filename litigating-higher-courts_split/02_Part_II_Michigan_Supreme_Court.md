## Part II — Michigan Supreme Court

### Overview

- Michigan Supreme Court is highest state court
- 7 justices (elected in nonpartisan elections)
- Must seek *leave to appeal* (not appeal of right in most cases)
- Grants leave only in cases of significant public interest, conflicts in COA, or substantial departures from controlling law

### Application for Leave to Appeal — MCR 7.305

**Filing Deadline:**
- **56 days** from COA opinion/order
- Delayed applications: within **6 months** with good cause

**Required Contents (MCR 7.302(C)):**
- Concise statement of facts
- Statement of questions presented
- Argument for why leave should be granted — must address MCR 7.302(D) factors:
  - Conflict with prior Supreme Court decisions
  - Conflict between COA opinions
  - Significant public interest
  - Substantial departures from law
  - Significant constitutional questions
- Copy of COA opinion
- Copy of trial court judgment
- Table of contents and authorities

**Tip:** Application for leave is a **persuasion document.** Do not just re-argue your appeal. Frame the issue as important beyond your case.

### Response to Application

- Appellee has **28 days** to file response
- May cross-apply within **21 days** of original application

### After Leave is Granted

1. Court issues order granting leave and specifying issues
2. Briefing schedule established
3. Principal briefs — same format as COA; **50 pages / 16,000 words**
4. Oral argument typically **30 minutes per side**
5. Decision — binding on all Michigan courts (stare decisis)

### Supreme Court Bypass (MCR 7.304)

- For cases of exceptional public importance
- Filed directly with Supreme Court, skipping COA
- Within **42 days** of final circuit court judgment
- Rarely granted — must show truly exceptional importance
