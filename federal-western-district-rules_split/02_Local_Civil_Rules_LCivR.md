## Local Civil Rules (LCivR)

### LCivR 1 — Scope and Purpose

- **LCivR 1.1** — Title
- **LCivR 1.2** — Effective Date
- **LCivR 1.3** — Scope and Applicability
  - All civil actions pending in Western District
  - Applicable to attorneys and parties
- **LCivR 1.4** — Sanctions for Noncompliance
  - Court may impose sanctions, dismiss, or enter default for failure to comply

### LCivR 3 — Commencement of Action

- **LCivR 3.1** — Filing; Case Opening
  - Actions commenced by filing complaint
  - Must use court's electronic filing system (CM/ECF)
  - Pro se filers may use paper until court order requires e-filing
- **LCivR 3.2** — Social Security Cases
  - Special procedures for Social Security appeals
  - Standard briefing schedule

### LCivR 5 — Service and Filing of Pleadings

- **LCivR 5.1** — Form of Papers
  - All papers must use:
    - 8½ × 11 paper
    - 12-point or larger font
    - Double-spacing (except block quotes, footnotes, tables)
    - 1-inch margins on all sides
    - Consecutive page numbers
    - Headers/footers identifying case
- **LCivR 5.2** — Electronic Filing (CM/ECF)
  - All attorneys must register for CM/ECF
  - Electronic filing is mandatory for attorneys
  - Service accomplished via CM/ECF Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF)
  - Technical difficulties — 24-hour window to file after system restored
- **LCivR 5.3** — Filing Under Seal
  - Motion to seal required
  - Must identify legal basis
  - Court scrutinizes sealing requests
- **LCivR 5.4** — Redaction Requirements
  - Social Security numbers: last 4 digits only
  - Financial account numbers: last 4 digits only
  - Dates of birth: year only
  - Names of minors: initials only
  - Home addresses of individuals: city and state only

### LCivR 7 — Motions

- **LCivR 7.1** — Motion Practice
  - Subrule (a): Movant must confer with opposing party and state in motion whether relief is opposed
  - Subrule (b): Brief required for dispositive motions; optional for non-dispositive motions unless ordered
  - Subrule (c): Page limits
    - Brief in support of motion: **25 pages**
    - Response brief: **25 pages**
    - Reply brief: **10 pages**
  - Subrule (d): No surreply without leave of court
  - Subrule (e): Hearing — court decides on briefs unless oral argument ordered
- **LCivR 7.2** — Summary Judgment Motions
  - Joint statement of undisputed facts may be required
  - Local summary judgment standards consistent with FRCP 56
- **LCivR 7.3** — Motions to Reconsider
  - Must be filed within **14 days** of order
  - Must show manifest error of law or newly discovered evidence
  - No new arguments that could have been raised before
  - **3-page limit** unless leave granted
- **LCivR 7.4** — Emergency Motions
  - Must certify urgency
  - Must describe attempts to confer
  - Court may schedule immediate hearing

### LCivR 10 — Form of Pleadings

- **LCivR 10.1** — Caption requirements
  - Court name, division
  - Names of all parties
  - Case number
  - Title of pleading
- **LCivR 10.2** — Attachments and Exhibits
  - Must be labeled with exhibit letters
  - Must be described in body of pleading
  - Voluminous exhibits may require separate filing

### LCivR 11 — Signing Documents

- All documents must be signed by attorney of record or pro se party
- Electronic signature (/s/ Name) acceptable in CM/ECF
- Attorney responsible for accuracy of all filings

### LCivR 15 — Amended Pleadings

- **LCivR 15.1** — Motion to Amend
  - Must attach proposed amended pleading as exhibit
  - Must identify all amendments

### LCivR 16 — Pretrial Procedures

- **LCivR 16.1** — Scheduling Order
  - Issued after FRCP 26(f) conference
  - Sets deadlines for:
    - Joinder of parties
    - Amendment of pleadings
    - Expert witness disclosures
    - Fact discovery close
    - Dispositive motion deadline
    - Final pretrial conference
    - Trial date
- **LCivR 16.2** — Final Pretrial Order
  - Required before trial in all cases
  - Identifies witnesses, exhibits, stipulations, contested issues
- **LCivR 16.3** — Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
  - Court encourages mediation and case evaluation
  - Parties may be ordered to participate in ADR
  - Western District Mediation Program available

### LCivR 26 — Discovery

- **LCivR 26.1** — Mandatory Disclosures
  - FRCP 26(a)(1) initial disclosures required within 14 days of FRCP 26(f) conference
- **LCivR 26.2** — Discovery Disputes
  - Parties must confer before filing discovery motion
  - Moving party must certify good-faith conferral efforts
  - Court may impose sanctions for failure to confer
- **LCivR 26.3** — Protective Orders
  - Stipulated protective orders may be filed
  - Must comply with public access policy
- **LCivR 26.4** — Electronically Stored Information (ESI)
  - Parties should address ESI in FRCP 26(f) conference
  - Form of production should be agreed upon early

### LCivR 37 — Failure to Make Disclosures; Sanctions

- **LCivR 37.1** — Discovery Motions — Conferral Requirement
  - Motion to compel must include certificate of conferral
  - Must identify specific discovery at issue
- **LCivR 37.2** — Sanctions
  - Court may award fees and costs
  - Extreme sanctions: dismissal, default, exclusion of evidence

### LCivR 41 — Dismissal

- **LCivR 41.1** — Want of Prosecution
  - Case may be dismissed if no activity for **1 year**
  - 28-day notice to parties before dismissal

### LCivR 47 — Jurors; Jury Trials

- **LCivR 47.1** — Juror Questions
  - Jurors may submit written questions
  - Court reviews questions; objections heard outside jury presence
- **LCivR 47.2** — Juror Note-Taking
  - Permitted in all civil trials
- **LCivR 47.3** — Juror Contact
  - No contact with jurors after verdict without court approval

### LCivR 54 — Judgments; Costs

- **LCivR 54.1** — Bill of Costs
  - Filed within **28 days** of judgment
  - Taxable costs per 28 U.S.C. § 1920
- **LCivR 54.2** — Attorneys' Fees
  - Motion for fees filed within **14 days** of judgment
  - Must include supporting documentation

### LCivR 56 — Summary Judgment

- Consistent with FRCP 56
- Statement of material facts:
  - Numbered paragraphs with record citations
  - Response must admit or deny each paragraph
  - Uncontested facts deemed admitted

### LCivR 65 — Injunctions

- Temporary restraining order (TRO) — FRCP 65(b)
  - May be issued ex parte upon proper showing
  - Limited to **14 days** unless extended
- Preliminary injunction
  - Requires hearing
  - Four-factor test: likelihood of success, irreparable harm, balance of harms, public interest

### LCivR 72 — Magistrate Judges

- **LCivR 72.1** — Referral to Magistrate Judge
  - Consent jurisdiction — all parties consent to full magistrate disposition
  - Referral of non-dispositive matters as of right
  - Referral of dispositive matters for R&R
- **LCivR 72.2** — Objections to Magistrate's Report & Recommendation
  - Filed within **14 days** of R&R
  - Specific objections required — general objections waive issue on appeal
- **LCivR 72.3** — Appeal of Non-Dispositive Rulings
  - Filed within **14 days**
  - Standard: clearly erroneous or contrary to law

### LCivR 73 — Trial by Magistrate Judge

- All parties must consent in writing
- Judgment entered by magistrate judge
- Appeal goes directly to Sixth Circuit

### LCivR 77 — Court Operations

- **LCivR 77.1** — Court Hours
- **LCivR 77.2** — Clerk's Office

### LCivR 79 — Sealed Records

- Procedures for filing under seal
- Public access presumption — sealing requires compelling reason

### LCivR 83 — Local Rules; Attorneys

- **LCivR 83.1** — Admission to Practice
  - Must be member of State Bar of Michigan
  - Or admitted pro hac vice
- **LCivR 83.2** — Pro Hac Vice Admission
  - Motion by local counsel required
  - $200 fee per case
  - Out-of-state attorney must associate with local counsel
- **LCivR 83.3** — Conduct of Attorneys
  - Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct apply
  - Court may impose discipline
- **LCivR 83.4** — Pro Se Parties
  - Pro se parties subject to same rules as attorneys
  - Liberal construction of pleadings (Haines v. Kerner)
- **LCivR 83.5** — Withdrawal of Appearance
  - Motion required after case is at-issue
  - Must certify client has been notified
